The Last Ice Age
Imagine a vast grassy ecosystem covered with herds of elephants, bison, and camels stretching as far as the eye can see.
Africa?
Maybe.
But this also describes Northern California at the end of the last Ice Age.
What happened to all this wildlife?
Were they over-hunted and killed off?
Did global warming destroy their populations?
Scientists are not sure, but this relatively recent loss of life does raise many interesting questions.

The Cenozoic Era is the age of mammals.
They evolved to fill virtually all the niches vacated by dinosaurs.
The ice ages of the Quaternary Period of the Cenozoic led to many extinctions.
The last ice age ended 12,000 years ago.
By that time, Homo sapiens had evolved.
