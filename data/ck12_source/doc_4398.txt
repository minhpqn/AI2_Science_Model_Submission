Flightless birds that are adapted for running include the ostrich, kiwi, rhea, cassowary, and moa.
Penguins are flightless birds adapted for swimming.

There are about 10,000 living species of birds, almost all of which can fly.
Flying birds are divided into 29 orders.
The most common orders include landfowl, waterfowl, shorebirds, diurnal and nocturnal raptors, parrots, and perching birds.
