DNA repair.
Shown is a model of DNA ligase repairing chromosomal damage.
DNA ligase is an enzyme that joins broken nucleotides together by catalyzing the formation of a bond between the phosphate group and deoxyribose sugar of adjacent nucleotides in the DNA backbone.
Cystic fibrosis is an example of a genetic disorder.
A mutation in a single gene causes the body to produce thick, sticky mucus that clogs the lungs and blocks ducts in digestive organs.

Cancer is a disease in which cells grow out of control and form abnormal masses of cells.
It is generally caused by mutations in genes that regulate the cell cycle.
Because of the mutations, cells with damaged DNA are allowed to divide without limits.
Cancer causing genes can be inherited.
