Relief , or terrain, includes all the landforms of a region.
For example, the image below shows the San Francisco Peaks in northern Arizona ( Figure below ).
Features on the map include mountains, hills, and lava flows.
You can recognize these features from the differences in elevation.
3D map of San Francisco Mountain and surrounding areas
This image was made from data of the Landsat satellite.
It shows the topography of the San Francisco Peaks and surrounding areas.
Elevation refers to height above average sea level.
The topography of a region describes its highs and lows.
Sea level is the midpoint between high and low tide.
It can vary around Earth.
