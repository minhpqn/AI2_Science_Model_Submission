Sedimentary rocks form when sediments are compacted and cemented together.
Sediments are pieces of rock.
They may be gravel, sand, silt, or clay.
Some sedimentary rocks form the solid minerals left behind after a liquid evaporates.
Metamorphic rocks form when an existing rock is changed by heat or pressure.
The minerals in the rock change but do not melt.
The rock experiences these changes within the Earth.
Rocks can change from one type to another.
The rock cycle describes how this happens.
Nearly all rocks are made of minerals.
A few are made of materials that do not fit the definition of minerals.
Igneous rocks form from cooled magma or lava.
Metamorphic rocks form as an existing rock is altered by high temperature or pressure.
