Insects are arthropods in the class Hexapoda.
They are the most numerous organisms in the world.
Most insects are terrestrial, and many are aerial.
Insects have six legs and a pair of antennae for sensing chemicals.
They also have several eyes and specialized mouthparts for feeding.
Insects are the only invertebrates than can fly.
Flight is the main reason for their success.
Insects may live in large colonies and have complex social behaviors.
Insects spread disease and destroy crops.
However, they are essential for pollinating flowering plants.
