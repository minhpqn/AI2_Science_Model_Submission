Homo sapiens arose about 200,000 years ago in Africa.
Early humans lived in small populations of nomadic hunters and gatherers.
They first left Africa about 40,000 years ago.
They soon moved throughout Europe, Asia, and Australia.
By 10,000 years ago, they had reached the Americas.
During this long period, birth and death rates were both fairly high.
As a result, population growth was slow.
Humans invented agriculture about 10,000 years ago.
This provided a bigger, more dependable food supply.
It also let them settle down in villages and cities for the first time.
The death rate increased because of diseases associated with domestic animals and crowded living conditions.
The birth rate increased because there was more food and settled life offered other advantages.
The combined effect was continued slow population growth.
