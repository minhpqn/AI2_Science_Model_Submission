carrier :
A person who is heterozygous for a recessive allele of a trait.
Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) :
Most sever form of muscular dystrophy; quickly worsens over time.
hemophilia :
The name of a group of hereditary genetic diseases that affect the body's ability to control blood clotting; characterized by a lack of clotting factors in the blood.
muscular dystrophy :
A group of inherited disorders that involve muscle weakness and loss of muscle tissue; progressively worsens over time.
sex-linked :
Located on a sex chromosome; usually refers to the X-chromosome.
SRY :
Sex-determining region Y; gene which encodes the testes-determining factor and triggers testis development, thus determining sex; located on the Y chromosome.
TSIX :
An XIST antisense transcript that repress the transcription of XIST, maintaining an active X chromosome.
X-inactivation :
The random inactivation of one X-chromosome in females; occurs early in embryonic development.
XIST :
X-inactive specific transcript; an RNA gene on the X chromosome of placental mammals; involved in the X inactivation process.
