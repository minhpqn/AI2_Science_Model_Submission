Even though bones stop growing in length in early adulthood, they can continue to increase in thickness throughout life.
This thickening occurs in response to strain from increased muscle activity and from weight-lifting exercises.
Bones, cartilage, and ligaments make up the skeletal system.
Functions of the skeletal system include providing support, protecting the soft organs of the body, aiding in movement, and making blood cells.
