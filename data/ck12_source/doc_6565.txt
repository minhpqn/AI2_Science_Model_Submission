Sunshine and Sleep
What does sunshine have to do with sleep (other than the sun shining in your eyes and waking you up)?
For many people, sleeping problems have to do with an amine that the brain makes called melatonin.
This compound affects the sleep-wake cycle and is affected by sunlight.
During the winter, the daily cycle of melatonin production may be affected by less sunlight hours.
If this cycle is changed, the person may have trouble sleeping.
Melatonin supplements are available in pharmacies and health food stores and may be of help for those with sleeping problems.
