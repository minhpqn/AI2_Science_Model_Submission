Fungi, along with bacteria that are found in soil, are the primary decomposers of organic matter in terrestrial ecosystems.
The decomposition of dead organisms returns nutrient to the soil, and the environment.
Fungi are a kingdom in the domain Eukarya that includes molds, mushrooms, and yeasts.
Most fungi are multicellular.
They are unique in having cell walls made of chitin.
Most fungi live on dead matter or soil.
Some live in aquatic habitats.
Many are involved in symbiotic relationships.
