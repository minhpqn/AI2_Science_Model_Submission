Genital Warts and Cervical Cancer
Infections with the human papillomavirus (HPV) are very common.
HPV may cause genital warts , which are small, rough growths on the genitals.
It may also cause cancer of the cervix in females.
A simple test, called a Pap test , can detect cervical cancer .
If the cancer is detected early, it usually can be cured with surgery.
There is also a vaccine , GARDASIL, to prevent infection with HPV.
The vaccine is recommended for females aged 11 to 26 years.
Viral STIs include genital herpes, hepatitis B, genital warts, and cervical cancer.
Viral STIs cannot be cured, but some of them can be prevented with vaccines.
