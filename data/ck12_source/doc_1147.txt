Can you see a lunar eclipse?
Again, of course!
Anyone with a view of the Moon can see a lunar eclipse.
The next four total lunar eclipses predicted for North America will be on April 15, 2014, October 8, 2014, April 5, 2015, and September 28, 2015.
Sometimes a full moon moves through Earth's shadow.
This is a lunar eclipse ( Figure below ).
During a total lunar eclipse, the Moon travels completely in Earth’s umbra.
During a partial lunar eclipse, only a portion of the Moon enters Earth’s umbra.
When the Moon passes through Earth’s penumbra, it is a penumbral eclipse.
Since Earth’s shadow is large, a lunar eclipse lasts for hours.
