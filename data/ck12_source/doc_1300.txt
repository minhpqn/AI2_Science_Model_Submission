A rock under enough stress will fracture.
There may or may not be movement along the fracture.
If there is not movement, the fracture is a joint.
If there is movement, the fracture is a fault.
If there is no movement on either side of a fracture, the fracture is called a joint .

These joints formed as the lava cooled and contracted.

If the rocks on one or both sides of a fracture move, the fracture is called a fault ( Figure below ).
Faults can occur alone or in clusters.
A cluster of faults creates a fault zone .
Earthquakes happen when rocks break and move suddenly.
The energy released causes an earthquake.
