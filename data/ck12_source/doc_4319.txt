Crinoids are essentially a mouth on the top surface that is surrounded by feeding arms.
Although the basic echinoderm pattern of fivefold symmetry can be recognized, most crinoids have many more than five arms.
Crinoids usually have a stem used to attach themselves to a surface, but many become free-swimming as adults.
