The remainder of mammals, which is the majority of mammals, are placental mammals.
These mammals develop in the mother's uterus, receiving nutrients across the placenta.
Placental mammals include humans, rabbits, squirrels, whales, elephants, shrews, and armadillos.
Dogs and cats, and sheep, cattle and horses are also placental mammals.
Mammals are also the only animal group that evolved to live on land and then back to live in the ocean.
Whales, dolphins, and porpoises have all adapted from land-dwelling creatures to a life of swimming and reproducing in the water ( Figure below ).
Whales have evolved into the largest mammals.
