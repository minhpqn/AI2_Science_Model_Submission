The solar system has eight planets:
Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.
There are also five known dwarf planets:
Ceres, Pluto, Makemake, Haumea, and Eris.
Solar system distances are measured as multiples of the distance between Earth and Sun.
This is one astronomical unit (AU).
All planets and dwarf planets orbit the Sun.
All planets and dwarf planets rotate on their axes.
The planets make slightly elliptical orbits around the Sun.
